Privacy Policy

HomeBrief

Effective April 2026

This document explains how HomeBrief handles information when you use the iOS and iPadOS application distributed on Apple’s App Store. It is written for anyone in your household who uses the app and should be read together with Apple’s own privacy materials for your device and account.

A Who this covers

The policy applies only to the HomeBrief mobile app on supported Apple devices. It does not describe other software, beta builds, or future products unless those releases include an updated policy inside the app or on this page.

B Data the developer does not collect

HomeBrief is labeled on the App Store with no data collection in the categories Apple asks developers to disclose there. In practical terms, the app is not designed to gather analytics identifiers, contact lists, precise location histories, or similar datasets for transmission to the developer through the app itself.

App Store labels can change when a version is published. Always check the latest App Privacy section on the HomeBrief store page before you update.

C What may stay on your device

Household briefs, checklists, notes, and readiness states you enter are expected to behave like ordinary on-device content: they exist so the app can show your morning overview and evening prep. Unless a future release clearly states otherwise in its description and privacy details, that material should remain under your control on the device storage Apple provides.

D Apple’s role

Downloading, updating, or signing in with an Apple ID involves processing by Apple Inc. under Apple’s terms, including billing, security, and device telemetry that Apple manages. HomeBrief does not replace or override those systems.

E Younger users

The App Store age band for HomeBrief is 4+. Parents and guardians should rely on Apple’s Family Sharing, Screen Time, and purchase controls to decide who may install or use the app on family devices.

F Keeping this text current

When the app or platform rules change in a way that affects privacy expectations, this page will be revised and the Effective date above will move forward. Continued use after an update means you accept the revised wording except where local law requires a different process.

If you need help with the product, use the standard App Store support path for the listing (for example ratings, reviews, or the support link Apple surfaces for that app)—this page intentionally does not include external sites or personal contact channels.
